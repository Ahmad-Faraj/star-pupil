# Star Pupil

Every AI product wants to teach you. This one asks you to do the teaching.

Star Pupil gives you a student. It knows nothing about your topic, and it will believe exactly what you tell it, including the things you explain badly. While you teach, you watch its understanding form on a live belief map. Explain something clearly and a node turns gold. Explain something sloppily and you get to watch a misconception take root in real time, with your own sentence quoted as the source.

Then comes the exam. Your pupil sits it alone, and it can only answer from what you taught. Every mark it loses traces back to a specific moment in your lesson, and if a wrong belief quietly built on top of an earlier one, the trace follows the chain back to the root, not just the nearest mistake. The report card it brings home is not grading the student. It also splits into two numbers: how much of the subject you actually covered, and how much of what you covered was right, because a confident 6/6 on half the topic is a different lesson than a shaky 6/6 on all of it.

The idea is old and good: the protege effect. Teaching something is the fastest way to find out you never understood it. Star Pupil just makes the evidence impossible to argue with. Talk to Pip instead of typing if you want, or skip straight to a finished report card from the enroll screen if you'd rather see the payoff before teaching your own lesson.

Built during a hackathon season, July 2026. Engine first, then the classroom.
